Chapter 1: The Beginning

Introduction

Chapter 1 is the original season of Fortnite, which set the foundation for the game's mechanics, progression, and popular features. It introduced several iconic weapons and items, as well as the concept of boss fights and map changes over time. Here's a breakdown of weapons added, weapons vaulted, and bosses that featured in Chapter 1:

Weapons

Weapons Added:


Fortnite Chapter 1 introduced a wide variety of weapons, many of which became staples of the game, while others were eventually vaulted. Here are some of the notable weapons that were added:

Assault Rifles:


Assault Rifle (Common, Uncommon, Rare): The basic, versatile weapon that could be used at both medium and long range.

Burst Assault Rifle (Uncommon, Rare): Fires in a burst of three shots, offering higher damage but slower rate of fire.

Scoped Assault Rifle (Epic, Legendary): An assault rifle with a built-in scope for long-range engagements.

Shotguns:


Pump Shotgun (Common, Uncommon, Rare): One of the most iconic weapons in Fortnite, known for its high damage at close range.

Tactical Shotgun (Common, Uncommon, Rare): A faster-firing shotgun with less damage per shot compared to the Pump Shotgun.

Heavy Shotgun (Epic, Legendary): A slower, but powerful shotgun that dealt high damage per shot at medium range.

Sniper Rifles:

Bolt-Action Sniper Rifle (Rare, Epic, Legendary): A one-shot kill weapon if aimed correctly, requiring a reload after each shot.

Semi-Auto Sniper Rifle (Uncommon, Rare, Epic): A faster-firing sniper rifle with lower damage per shot.

Hunting Rifle (Rare): A bolt-action sniper with no scope, but faster to fire compared to the Bolt-Action Sniper Rifle.

SMGs:

Submachine Gun (Common, Uncommon, Rare): A fast-firing weapon for close to medium range combat.

Silenced Submachine Gun (Epic, Legendary): A stealthier version of the regular SMG with reduced noise and higher accuracy.

Pistols:

Revolver (Common, Uncommon): A high-damage pistol with slower fire rate.

Silenced Pistol (Common, Uncommon): A stealthier variant of the pistol.

Hand Cannon (Epic, Legendary): A powerful pistol that dealt significant damage, especially at close range.

Explosives:

Rocket Launcher (Epic, Legendary): Fires explosive rockets, dealing massive damage to structures and enemies.

Grenades (Common): Standard grenades that deal damage in an area of effect after a delay.

C4 (Rare): A remote-detonated explosive used to destroy structures and deal damage.

Other Notable Weapons:

Crossbow (Epic): A silent, long-range weapon that dealt damage and could be used for stealthy eliminations.

Minigun (Epic, Legendary): A rapid-fire machine gun with massive ammo capacity.

Mounted Turret (Epic): A stationary machine gun that dealt heavy damage but could only be used while mounted.



Weapons Vaulted:


Over the course of Chapter 1, Epic Games frequently vaulted certain weapons to keep the game balanced and fresh. Some of the notable vaulted weapons included:


Tactical Assault Rifle (Epic, Legendary): A burst-fire rifle added in Chapter 1 but later vaulted due to balance concerns.

Chug Jug (Legendary): A healing item that restored full health and shield, but was later vaulted due to its strong healing properties.

Dual Pistols (Epic, Legendary): A pair of pistols that allowed players to rapidly fire at close range but were eventually vaulted for balance reasons.

Infinity Blade (Mythic): A powerful melee weapon that was introduced during special events but was soon vaulted due to its overpowered nature.

Suppressed Sniper Rifle (Epic): A quieter sniper rifle that dealt less damage than the Bolt-Action, but it was vaulted to ensure better balance.

Bosses

While boss fights in Fortnite were a much more recent addition in later chapters, Chapter 1 did not feature boss characters in the way we see them today. However, certain limited-time events and special challenges did introduce powerful NPCs or enemies that could be considered "boss-like." Some of the key moments in Chapter 1's timeline involving unique, powerful foes include:



The Cube and its Guardians (Season 5): The mysterious Cube (Kevin) introduced during Season 5 had a range of interactions with the environment, including summoning powerful enemies and altering the landscape in various ways. The Cube didn't technically have a "boss," but its influence on the environment added a layer of complexity and mystery to the game.

Fortnite "Events" Boss Fights: Epic did introduce some NPC bosses in special events, such as Season 4's Thanos Event, where Thanos from Marvel's Avengers universe came to the game as a special boss character. Players could battle him for the chance to wield the powerful Infinity Gauntlet.
